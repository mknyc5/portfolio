Project Name: Solar Hydrogen Vehicle Project

Course: Principles of Engineering

Project Duration: 11/1/17 - 11/17/17

Project Type: Group

Objective: Design, Build, and Test a Vehicle powered by either solar or hydrogen fuel cells

The Solar Hydrogen Vehicle Project was a three person project that required a recommendation for Tesla Motors, regarding whether they should use solar or hydrogen fuel and how they should wire the cells on a bread board. For this project our group had to test various configurations of fuel cells on a vehicle of our design for efficiency, speed, and practicality. During the project, I took lead on documentation and building the vehicle. I documented all of our modifications and recorded our vehicle's time results. After trying many different combinations of hydrogen cells and solar panels, we ultimately used 2 hydrogen cells, configured in a parallel circuit. After our testing we concluded that the hydrogen fuel cells produced the greatest speed. However practicality of our source is low, because hydrogen fuel cells are still very inefficient, and are heavy. In todays market, hydrogen fuel cars are not widely advertised due to its inefficiencies. Furthermore, our inability to obtain functioning solar panels may have skewed our results. However, based off our group's experiences with hydrgoen fuel cells and solar panels, we ultimately recommend 2 hydrogen fuel cells wired in parallel to Tesla.

Course: Computer Science Principles

Project Duration: 8/30/18 - 9/15/18

Project Type: Partner

-->

Objective: Design a storyline or a game using the scratch interface

The Scratch Project was a two person project that involved using the interface Scratch. For this project we had to choose to develop either a story or a game. My partner, Keenan, and I decided to create a game that fuses the concepts of a storyline and game. It centered around a student named Mike who did not want to be at school. Mike then interacts with the user, asking for his/her name and asks if he/she would like to escape school with him. Once the user says yes, they must help Mike escape the teacher and school by controlling him to avoid obstacles that come in the way. As he gets closer to home, the obstacles become harder and more freqeuent. The game ends after surviving a certain amount of loops that we defined as levels. During this project I learned how to work in a team while programming as well as how to change our game for the benefit of the players of the game. A main component of this game was variables and globals since there was a lot of data to keep track of, and a need to be able to transition into a different level.
